Cookie Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2024  |  Last Updated: January 1, 2025

In plain English: We use a small number of cookies to make our website function correctly and to understand how visitors use it. We do not use advertising or tracking cookies. You can control cookie preferences through your browser settings.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. They allow the website to recognize your browser, remember your preferences, and collect information about your visit to improve your experience.

Similar technologies include web beacons (tiny images embedded in pages), local storage, and session storage. This policy covers all such technologies collectively referred to as "cookies."

2. How We Use Cookies

Evolvity Solutions uses cookies on www.evolvity.com for the following purposes:

  • To ensure our website functions correctly and securely.
  • To remember your preferences during a browsing session.
  • To analyze how visitors use our Site so we can improve the experience.
  • To measure the effectiveness of our content.

We do not use cookies for behavioral advertising, cross-site tracking, or the sale of your browsing data.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

Cookie Name / Category Type Purpose Duration
Session state Essential Maintains basic website functionality and security during your visit. Session (deleted when browser closes)
CSRF protection token Essential Protects form submissions from cross-site request forgery attacks. Session
Google Analytics (_ga, _gid) Analytics Collects anonymized data on page views, session duration, and traffic sources to help us improve content and navigation. IP addresses are anonymized. _ga: 2 years; _gid: 24 hours
User preference Functional Remembers settings such as language or display preferences between visits. 1 year

4. Essential Cookies

Essential cookies are strictly necessary for our website to operate. They enable core functions such as page navigation, form submission security, and load balancing. Without these cookies, the website cannot function properly. Because they are required for the Site to work, essential cookies cannot be disabled through our cookie controls.

5. Analytics Cookies

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors interact with our Site—which pages are visited, how long visitors stay, and how they navigate between sections. This data is aggregated and anonymized; it cannot be used to identify you personally.

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking at any time by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

6. Third-Party Cookies

Some pages on our Site may include embedded content or links from third-party services (for example, LinkedIn, YouTube, or social sharing buttons). These third parties may place their own cookies on your device. We do not control these cookies and recommend reviewing the relevant third-party privacy and cookie policies.

7. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

7.1 Browser Settings

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies, and to be notified before a cookie is placed. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our Site.

Browser-specific instructions:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

7.2 Do Not Track

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. Our Site currently does not alter its behavior based on DNT signals, though we limit our data collection to the minimal set described in this policy. We will update this policy if our practices change.

8. Consent

By continuing to use our Site without changing your browser's cookie settings, you consent to our use of cookies as described in this policy. You may withdraw your consent at any time by adjusting your browser settings or opting out of analytics as described in Section 5.

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA) or United Kingdom, we will seek your explicit consent before placing non-essential cookies, in compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the UK Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR).

9. Data Collected via Cookies

Data collected via analytics cookies may include:

  • Pages visited and time spent on each page.
  • Referring website (how you arrived at our Site).
  • Browser type, version, and operating system.
  • Device type (desktop, tablet, mobile).
  • General geographic location (country/region — not precise location).
  • Interactions such as clicks and scroll depth.

This data is never linked to any personally identifiable information and is processed in accordance with our Privacy Policy.

10. Updates to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy periodically to reflect changes in our use of cookies or applicable law. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
